# Further reading

Every link below was checked to make sure it resolves and actually matches
its description before going on this page.

### The original paper

Dan Boneh, Joseph Bonneau, Benedikt Bünz, and Ben Fisch, ["Verifiable Delay
Functions"](https://eprint.iacr.org/2018/601) (CRYPTO 2018, pp. 757–788).
The paper this whole site is built around: the formal Verifiable/Delay/
Function definition, the repeated-squaring construction, and the original
application ideas.

### The two independent proof systems

- Benjamin Wesolowski, ["Efficient Verifiable Delay
  Functions"](https://eprint.iacr.org/2018/623) (EUROCRYPT 2019, pp.
  379–407). The proof scheme this site's own [demo](/interactive)
  implements.
- Krzysztof Pietrzak, ["Simple Verifiable Delay
  Functions"](https://eprint.iacr.org/2018/627) (ITCS 2019, LIPIcs vol. 124,
  pp. 60:1–60:15). The faster-to-generate, larger-proof alternative — see
  [how it works](/how-it-works) for how the two tradeoffs compare.

### Comparing the two

Dan Boneh, Benedikt Bünz, and Ben Fisch, ["A Survey of Two Verifiable Delay
Functions"](https://eprint.iacr.org/2018/712) (IACR ePrint 2018/712). Puts
Wesolowski's and Pietrzak's schemes side by side with a unified security
analysis — the clearest single source for *why* someone would pick one over
the other.

### A production deployment

[Chia Network — Proof of Time (VDFs)](https://docs.chia.net/proof-of-time/).
Chia's own documentation for the class-group-based VDF at the center of its
consensus protocol — see [Applications](/applications) for how this site
summarizes it.
